Feature Scaling



K-Means is distance-based. Here 'income' is in thousands, and 'health' is between 0 and 18.
If we don't scale, a large number of characters (income, gdpp) completely take over the result.
'StandardScaler' converts all characters to the same size (mean=0, std=1).

Finding the best K (number of clusters)



In K-Means, we select the number of clusters (K) ourselves. We use two methods:

Elbow method - where the graph of inertia (dispersion within a cluster) creates an "elbow"

Silhouette score - shows how well clusters are separated (high = good)
In [38]:
inertias = []
silhouettes = []
k_range = range(2, 11)

for k in k_range:
    km = KMeans(n_clusters=k, random_state=42, n_init=10)
    km.fit(X_scaled)
    inertias.append(km.inertia_)
    silhouettes.append(silhouette_score(X_scaled, km.labels_))

fig, axes = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(14, 5))

axes[0].plot(k_range, inertias, marker="o", color="purple")
axes[0].set_title("Elbow method (Inertia)")
axes[0].set_xlabel("K")
axes[0].set_ylabel("Inertia")

axes[1].plot(k_range, silhouettes, marker="o", color="green")
axes[1].set_title("Silhouette score")
axes[1].set_xlabel("K")
axes[1].set_ylabel("Silhouette")

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
 
Analysis: On the Elbow graph, the "elbow" appears to be around K=3. K=3 business point of view
from the perspective of the most logical - it divides countries into 3 groups: developed,
developing, in need of help. Therefore, we choose K=3.
